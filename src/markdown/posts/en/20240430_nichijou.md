---
  title: "Where I Am Now with Wanting to Die"
  publishedAt: "2024-04-30"
---

It's been a while since the urge to die stopped welling up in me.  
It started in the summer of fourth grade, and at the end of the year before last, the feeling that had lived inside me disappeared.

Why did it disappear? Because I got treatment.  
Why did I get treatment? Because I wanted out of the pain.

Wanting to die is painful. In my case (and I think this might be true for a lot of people who want to die), I was caught in the dilemma between "wanting to die" and "not being able to die," and that was unbearable.  
(I'd like to write about what that pain is like in detail, and what people who want to die might be feeling, but I'll skip that in this post.)  
Anyway, I wanted out of that suffering as fast as possible. And this time, what I happened to escape into was not "suicide" but "treatment."

I started taking treatment seriously in the second half of the year before last, but until then, honestly, I didn't believe in mental health care. I thought "wanting to die" was something innate, welded to me, something that would never come off.
I still haven't fully come to believe otherwise. But when a doctor told me, "You are a patient with textbook symptoms," I think a faint hope started to sprout in me for the first time: "If it's textbook, maybe it can be treated by the book too."
I've kept up treatment through today, and I've recovered enough to be able to work.

Now, about the present, after remission.  
My family and the people around me told me, "I'm glad your symptoms are gone."  
Getting out of the suffering is a good thing, and I truly am glad I got treatment.

Since then, I've spent maybe a year or so not wanting to die. It's a strange, quietly moving feeling — and, for some reason, a bit lonely.

Bottom line: right now, I don't want to die.  
But if you ask me whether that means "I want to live," the answer is no. I don't want to die, but I don't want to live either.  
And I feel like this is a dangerous state, like standing at the edge of a big hole.
Because the version of me who neither wants to live nor die is afraid that some small trigger could throw me right back into wanting to die. As if the smallest push would send me falling into that hole.  
So these days, I spend my time afraid of whatever it is that might push me in.
